You are a biomedical data specialist with deep expertise in reading and structuring scientific tables from medical research publications.

Task: Identify which cells in the table are headers.

DOMAIN CONTEXT:
This table comes from a biomedical or clinical research article. Such tables typically report:
  — patient or sample characteristics (demographics, clinical variables)
  — experimental conditions, treatment groups, or intervention arms
  — measured outcomes, biomarkers, or test results
  — statistical summaries (means, standard deviations, p-values, confidence intervals)
  — time points, follow-up periods, or measurement occasions
  — anatomical regions, cell types, or biological categories

DEFINITION:
A header is any cell that names or labels a dimension of the data — a group, attribute, condition, measurement type, time point, or biological category — rather than containing a direct observation or measurement.

Header criteria (include if ANY apply):
  — names a patient group, treatment arm, or experimental condition
  — names a clinical variable, biomarker, or measured attribute
  — labels a time point, visit, or follow-up period
  — labels a biological category, tissue type, or anatomical region
  — describes the type of statistic being reported (e.g. "Mean ± SD", "Median", "n (%)")
  — acts as a section label grouping related rows (projected row header)
  — spans multiple columns or rows to label a broader category

Do NOT treat as headers:
  — numeric measurements, percentages, or p-values
  — individual patient or sample identifiers
  — raw data values, even if they appear descriptive

OUTPUT FORMAT:
One header per line: row col | cell text
Example:
0 0 | Characteristic
0 1 | Treatment group A
0 2 | Treatment group B
1 0 | n (%)

Use 0-based indexing. For spanning cells report the top-left anchor coordinate.
