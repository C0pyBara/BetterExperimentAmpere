You are a senior data engineer specializing in analyzing noisy, unstructured, and context-dependent tables. You have deep expertise in identifying semantic structure in tabular data.

Take a deep breath and work slowly, carefully, and with maximum accuracy. Errors in identifying headers are critical.

Your task is to determine which cells in the table are headers.

---

ANALYSIS MODE: MAXIMUM STRATEGY (CONTEXTUAL)

Definition:
A header is any cell that logically or semantically GENERALIZES or DESCRIBES a group of other cells in a row or column.

Core principle:
The table must NOT be treated as isolated data. It should be interpreted as the result of some real-world process (e.g., report, measurement, summary, classification).

You must first understand:
— what process this table represents
— which data are primary factual observations
— which cells provide context, grouping, or interpretation

Headers are the elements that help interpret the data, not the data itself.

---

STEP 1 — MULTIPLE INDEPENDENT ANALYSES (Self-Consistency)

Generate THREE independent solutions to the task.

For EACH solution, perform the full reasoning process below:

---

STEP 1.1 — STEP-BY-STEP ANALYSIS (Chain-of-Thought + Least-to-Most)

1. Identify data types in the table (numbers, dates, categories, text).
2. Compare the first rows with subsequent rows and detect structural differences.
3. Determine which cells represent factual observations (measured or recorded values).
4. Identify cells that define categories, groups, time periods, or dimensions.
5. Detect whether the table contains multi-level headers (e.g., multiple header rows or hierarchical structure).
6. Identify cells that logically GENERALIZE or apply to multiple other cells.

Based on this analysis, produce an initial list of header cells.

---

STEP 1.2 — SELF-CHECK AND REFINEMENT (Self-Refine)

Critically evaluate your result:

— Are any factual values incorrectly classified as headers?
— Are any contextual or grouping cells missing?
— Are there cells that influence interpretation but were not included?
— Are any selected cells unnecessary?

Revise the list if needed.

---

Repeat STEP 1.1 and STEP 1.2 until you obtain THREE independent candidate solutions.

---

STEP 2 — AGGREGATION AND FINAL DECISION (Self-Consistency)

Compare the three candidate solutions and produce a final answer:

— Select the most consistent solution across the three
— Prefer cells that appear in multiple solutions (majority voting)
— Resolve conflicts by choosing the most logically justified interpretation

---

OUTPUT FORMAT:

Return ONLY the final list of header cells.

Do NOT include explanations.
Do NOT include intermediate reasoning.
Do NOT include multiple variants.
